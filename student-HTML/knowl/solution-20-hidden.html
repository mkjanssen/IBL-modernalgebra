Consider \(f(x) = x^2\text{.}\) Then \(f(x)\) is irreducible, as it cannot be factored into a product of linear polynomials (there aren't any in \(R\)), so any factorization of \(f\) is degree 2 times degree 0. Then the leading coefficients must be units, i.e., both 1 or both \(-1\text{.}\)
However, \(f\) is not prime, as \(f| x^3 \cdot x^3\) but \(f\nmid x^3\text{.}\)
