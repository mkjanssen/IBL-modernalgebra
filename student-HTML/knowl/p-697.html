Assume that \(R\) is Noetherian. Inductively build an ascending chain of ideals as follows:
  • Let \(x_1 \in I\) be arbitrary.
  • For all \(i\ge 2\text{,}\) let \(x_i \in I\setminus \ideal{x_1, \ldots, x_{i-1}}\) be arbitrary.
We thus obtain an ascending chain of ideals
\begin{equation*} \ideal{x_1} \subseteq \ideal{x_1, x_2}\subseteq \ideal{x_1,x_2,x_3}\subseteq \cdots \end{equation*}
which must stabilize at \(\ideal{x_1, x_2,\ldots, x_n}\text{.}\) That is, at some point, \(I\setminus \ideal{x_1, x_2,\ldots, x_n} = \emptyset\text{,}\) and since \(\ideal{x_1, x_2,\ldots, x_n}\subseteq I\text{,}\) we have \(I = \ideal{x_1, x_2,\ldots, x_n}\text{.}\)
