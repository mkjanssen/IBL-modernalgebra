In the 1920s, Emmy Noether was the first to explicitly describe the ring axioms as we know them today, and her definition of a (not-necessarily-commutative) ring has led to a great deal of interesting work in algebra, number theory, and geometry, including the (see Section 3.3 for more on the historical development of the proof of Fermat's Last Theorem). Most modern definitions of ring agree with our Definition 2.2.6 and allow for rings with noncommutative multiplication and no multiplicative identity.
