Definition 1.4.8.

Let \(m > 1\) and \(a,b\in \Z_m\text{.}\) Then the following are well-defined operations on the equivalence classes:

  1. Addition modulo \(m\): \(\overline{a} + \overline{b} := \overline{a+b}\text{.}\)

    The symbol \(:=\) is often used to indicate that we are defining the expression on the left to equal the expression on the right.

  2. Multiplication modulo \(m\): \(\overline{a}\cdot \overline{b} := \overline{a\cdot b}\text{.}\)
