Theorem 1.4.17.

Let \(p\in \N\) be prime and \(\overline{a},\overline{b},\overline{c}\in \Z_p\) such that \(\overline{c}\ne \overline{0}\text{.}\) Then

  1. there is some \(\overline{x}\in \Z_p\) such that \(\overline{c}\cdot \overline{x} = \overline{1}\text{;}\) and,

  2. if \(\overline{a} \cdot\overline{c} = \overline{b}\cdot\overline{c}\text{,}\) \(\overline{a} = \overline{b}\text{.}\)
