Proof

Let \(a\in R\) be prime, and suppose that \(a = bc\) for some \(b,c\in R\text{.}\) Then \(a|bc\text{,}\) so by definition either \(a|b\) or \(a|c\text{.}\) Without loss of generality, assume \(a|b\text{.}\) Then there is a \(k\in R\) such that \(ak=b\text{,}\) so \(a = (ak)c\text{,}\) and we may cancel \(a\) to obtain \(1_R = kc\text{.}\) Thus, \(c\) is a unit, making \(a\) irreducible.
