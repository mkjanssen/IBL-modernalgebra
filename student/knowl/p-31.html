Let \(P(m)\) be a statement about the natural number \(m\) 2 . Let \(k_0\in \N\) be such that the statement \(P(k_0)\) is true (the base case), and suppose there is an \(n\ge k_0\) such that for all \(k\) satisfying \(k_0 \le k \le n\text{,}\) \(P(k)\) is true (the inductive hypothesis). Then \(P(n+1)\) is true, and thus \(P(m)\) is true for all \(m\ge k_0\) (the inductive step).
