For the reverse, assume \(R\) is a domain. Then \(R\) has no zero divisors. If \(m\) is composite, there exist integers \(a,b\) satisfying \(1 \lt a,b \lt m\) such that \(m=ab\text{.}\) Then \(\overline{a},\overline{b}\ne \overline{0}\) in \(\Z_m\text{,}\) but \(\overline{a}\cdot \overline{b} = \overline{0}\text{.}\) Thus, \(m\) may not be composite, and is therefore prime. By an earlier theorem, \(\Z_p\) is a field.
