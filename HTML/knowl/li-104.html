Item 6.

Observe that \((-1) + 1 = 0\) by the definition of additive inverses. Multiply both sides by \(-1\) and distribute to obtain \((-1)(-1) + (-1)\cdot 1 = (-1)\cdot 0\text{.}\) By part 2 of this theorem and the definition of a multiplicative identity, we obtain \((-1)(-1) + (-1) = 0\text{.}\) We now add the multiplicative identity to obtain \((-1)(-1) + (-1) + 1 = 0 + 1\text{,}\) which simplifies to \((-1)(-1) = 1\text{.}\)
