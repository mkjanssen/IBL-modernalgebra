Most elementary propositions about \(\Z_m\) can be recast as statements about \(\Z\text{.}\) For instance, in proving Theorem 1.4.10 you likely proved that if \(m|a-c\) and \(m|b-d\) that \(m|(a+b)-(c+d)\text{.}\) However, as the statements become more complex, repeatedly reshaping statements about \(Z_m\) as statements about \(\Z\) becomes cumbersome and unhelpful. Instead, you are encouraged to become comfortable doing arithmetic modulo \(m\) or, put another way, arithmetic with the equivalence classes of \(\Z_m\) as defined in Definition 1.4.13.
