The notion of a relation as presented above is extremely open-ended. Any subset of ordered pairs of \(S\times S\) describes a relation on the set \(S\text{.}\) Of course, some relations are more meaningful than others; the branch of mathematics known as order theory 1  studies order relations (such as the familiar \(\lt\)). Our focus will be on equivalence relations, which isolate the important features of \(=\text{.}\)
