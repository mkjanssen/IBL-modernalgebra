Definition 2.2.1.

Let \(A\) be a set, and \(x\) a variable. We define a polynomial in \(x\) with coefficients in \(A\) to be an expression of the form

\begin{equation*} p = a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + \cdots + a_n x^n\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(n\in \N_0\) is the degree of the polynomial \(p\text{,}\) denoted \(\deg(p) = n\text{,}\) and \(a_0,a_1,\ldots, a_n\) are the coefficients of the polynomial. The coefficient \(a_n\) is known as the leading coefficient of \(p\text{,}\) and \(a_n x^n\) is the leading term of \(p\text{.}\) By

\begin{equation*} A[x] := \setof{a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x + \cdots + a_n x^n}{n\in \N_0, \ a_i\in A} \end{equation*}

we denote the set of all polynomials with coefficients in \(A\text{.}\) If \(A\) has an additive identity \(0\text{,}\) then \(A[x]\) has a zero polynomial, the polynomial whose coefficients are all \(0\text{.}\) The zero polynomial is also often denoted by \(0\text{.}\) The degree of the zero polynomial is \(-\infty\text{.}\)
