In 1847, Gabriel Lam√© claimed he had completely solved the problem. His solution relied on the factorization of \(x^p + y^p\text{,}\) where \(p\) is an odd prime, as

\begin{equation*} x^p + y^p = (x+y)(x+\zeta y) \cdots (x+\zeta^{p-1}y)\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\zeta = e^{2\pi i/p}\) is a primitive \(p\)-th root of unity in \(\C\text{.}\) However, the ring \(\Z[\zeta] = \setof{a_0 + a_1 \zeta + a_2 \zeta^2 + \cdots + a_{p-1} \zeta^{p-1}}{a_i\in\Z}\) is not a unique factorization domain.
